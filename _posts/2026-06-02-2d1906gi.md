Sarah Albu & Annika Fuhrmann  
  
Experimental vocalist, soprano and performance-maker Sarah Albu (Montreal) plays with ever-shifting vocal textures and emotional extremes to explore a multitude of characters. Annika Fuhrmann (Helsinki) is a vocalist and sound artist working across experimental music theatre, sound art, and improvisation. Her practice is known for its expressive vocal and bodily approach, often engaging with social conscious themes.

Mercedes Krapovickas  
  
Mercedes Krapovickas is a bandoneon performer, composer, and artistic researcher based in Finland. She is currently a doctoral candidate at the Sibelius Academy, University of the Arts Helsinki, where her research explores the bandoneon as an extended, relational instrument within contemporary performance practices. Her work combines acoustic performance, live electronics, and spatial sound, focusing on the interaction between body, movement, and technology.   
  
[https://mekrapov.com/](https://mekrapov.com/)  
  
--  
  
Charles Quevillon  
  
Charles Quevillon is a Canadian-Finnish composer and performer. His practice is centered on the physicality of music performace, where sound-making often extends into other media such as dance, theater, and circus. He also likes to touch upon themes like ritual, the sacred, technology, and consumption.
