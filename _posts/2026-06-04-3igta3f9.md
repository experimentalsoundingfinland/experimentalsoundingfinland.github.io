Spring season of Suhina Klubi ends with a bang,  when Chris Corsano, Camila Nebbia & Jukka Kääriäinen take the stage on 4th of June  
  
Described as "one of the world's greatest drummers" by The Guardian and "arguably the most riotously energetic and creative drummer in contemporary free jazz" by The Wire Magazine, New York-based 𝗖𝗵𝗿𝗶𝘀 𝗖𝗼𝗿𝘀𝗮𝗻𝗼 (b. 1975) has been active at the intersections of collective improvisation, free jazz, avant-rock, and experimental music since the late 90's. His dedication to collective improvisation has led him to join forces with many kindred spirits, and he's appeared on over 180 records and thousands of live performances. Besides his renown solo career, Corsano's been the rim-batterer of choice for some of the greatest contemporary purveyors of jazz (Joe McPhee, Paul Flaherty, Mette Rasmussen, Zoh Amba) and rock (Sir Richard Bishop, Bill Orcutt, Jim O'Rourke), as well as artists beyond categorization (Björk for her Volta World Tour, Michael Flower, Okkyung Lee).  
  
Berlin-based 𝗖𝗮𝗺𝗶𝗹𝗮 𝗡𝗲𝗯𝗯𝗶𝗮 is known as a saxophone player, composer, improviser, visual artist and curator. Described by Jazz PT magazine as “an essential saxophonist of our time”, she's been nominated for the German Jazz Prize (Woodwind-category) in 2024 & 2025. She has performed with artists such as Marilyn Crispell, Michael Formanek, Angelica Sanchez, Lesley Mok, Kit Downes, Andrew Lisle, John Edwards, and Hamid Drake. Part of her current projects are: Nebbia/Downes/Lisle "Exhaust", Camila Nebbia & Angelica Sanchez Duo, Nebbia Formaneck Sperrazza trio, Camila Nebbia's The Hanged One sextet, "Presencia".   
  
The trio's last musician, Suhina Klubi's Jukka Kääriäinen, is a musician focused on experimental music, who's working with prepared acoustic guitar or electric guitar and effects. He has performed widely across Europe and at international festivals in the UK, Spain, and France.
